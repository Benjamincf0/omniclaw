    Community: Library Renovation Project
    Published on June 12, 2017 at 14:40, updated on June 12, 2017 at 14:48

    Library locations during renovations

    As you may have heard, we have been assessing possible solutions to provide limited library services over the next 2-years as the renovations take place. The library presently provides over 4000 square-meters, the majority of which is study space for students. Our initial plan was to use our 3 "buffer classrooms", and then to add portables behind the Penfield building (as had been done on the past) to provide the remaining required space.

    We were recently informed that new building &fire codes do not allow us to connect portables to the college. The portables would need to be installed on the grass circle in front of the college (near Brittain Hall) at a cost of approximately 500k$. This is prohibitive, and we thus decided to save these funds to improve the library rather than adding temporary and inconvenient space. McGill-Macdonald Campus, and Macdonald HS have also informed us that they cannot provide additional usable space. We have therefore decided to do our best with available spaces around the college.

    Other than the buffer classrooms, we have decided to use the following spaces for library services, starting this summer, for the duration of the Library renovations (estimated at 18 to 24-months):
    a) The Board Room (SH-104); the Faculty-Staff Dining Room (SH-101B) in the cafeteria; and the Faculty-Staff Lounge (H-101). All activities that normally take place in these rooms will need to be rescheduled to other locations.  We are also planning to install computers in the following spaces to help increase the available study space and access to the electronic collection: the study room in front of the bookstore (SH-112); the student lounge on the ground floor of the AME building (AME-120); and the student lounge on the 1st floor of the Casgrain (C-187). Even with all these spaces, the library will be operating on approximately 25% of its present space, which is far from ideal.

    We are very sorry for the inconveniences that this will bring.  However, the architectural concepts that we have seen clearly show that this short-term pain will provide great and much-needed improvements to our infrastructures. Thank you for your continued collaboration and understanding.

     

    Regards

    John Halpin

    LIBRARY RENOVATION PROJECT

    Back in December, Director General John Halpin proudly announced the College would be receiving $7.2 million from the Federal government ’s Post-Secondary Institutions Strategic Investment Fund and from the Quebec government for the renovation of our Library. Since then, Catherine Scheer (FMS) and her team have been working with the Library team, the Library Project Group and the Library Users Group to plan this project.

    As you may have heard, on April 13, a selection committee met and chose the firm Émond, Kozina, Mulvey, architects, s.e.n.c.r.l. (EKM) to design and oversee the Library Renovation Project. This firm was selected based on their experience in university libraries, their expertise in heritage restoration and, ultimately, the preliminary concept they presented. This concept emphasizes the interaction between the varied learning spaces while allowing more natural light in and preserving the heritage features of the library.

    For the upcoming exam period (May 11-19), the library will be open from 8:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m., Monday to Friday. The media section will be open from 8:00 a.m. to 4:00 p.m. during those days.

    Starting this summer, Library Media Services will be relocated to other areas of the campus and staff, services and resources will be accessible during the entire renovation period.

    Like in any grand renovation project, we can expect minor disruptions but we will do our best to minimize them.

    We will keep you posted throughout the Library Renovation Project. A new library! Well that ’s something to get excited about!
